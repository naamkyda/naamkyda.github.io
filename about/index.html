About

Hello stranger!

Welcome to journaling corner of naamkyda – a space for investigations, discoveries, reflections, and insights that go beyond the purely technical.

For more than a decade, I have spent my time helping companies battle digital machines — both their own when they malfunctioned, and those that could invade them from the outside. Over the years, I have faced countless hair-pulling, jaw-dropping, head-against-the-wall moments. Yet with every problem solved, every challenge overcome, and every vulnerability explored, I became a little wiser and more knowledgeable. I often thought about sharing these little fragments publicly, but never found the time, and thus they were pitifully forgotten. The same goes for many other topics beyond computers.

Therefore, the main purpose of this place is to change that — to start preserving interesting thoughts, problems, solutions, investigations, and more, and share them with the world. I hope they can help and inspire others, just as similar places have inspired me many times on my journey.

With that being said, this place is not limited to stories about computers… Or is it? We are all, after all, some sort of programmable, highly intelligent, feeling machines — capable of things that are sometimes amazing, sometimes terrifying. From early childhood our desires are shaped by the world around us, as we are becoming destined for specific behavioral patterns of action and reaction. These are complex, yet very simple, the closer we look. We chase happiness with all our might, camouflaged in thousands of different forms, yet so often fail to see what is already there.

The question is…where do we go?
